Introduction to Microeconomics

Syllabus | Email

Economists ask, “What choices should we make when we can’t have everything?” Each of us make hundreds of choices every day as an individual and as a member of a larger social and political environment. This class is about individual choices and how those choices interact in groups. Tools in this class organize your thinking about decisions we face as individuals and as a society.


Part A The Landscape
Part A introduces the foundational ideas in the economics landscape.
Part B Coordination Using Markets
Part B introduces markets as a way to coordinate buyers and sellers in large groups.
Part C Market Failures
Part C explores some cases where markets don't deliver what's best for society, what we call "Market Failures".
Part D Government Policies
Part D introduces some government policy options to fix market failures and help reach other social objectives.
Part E Sellers
Part E explores in more detail how sellers can maximize their choices across a range of market environments.
Part F Buyers
Part F explores in more detail how buyers can maximize their choices.

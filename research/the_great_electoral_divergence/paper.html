ABOUT ME


I am a PhD student in the Department of Economics, at the University of Pittsburgh.

I specialize in public, urban, behavioral, and political economy. My main research interests focus on belief formation, equalibrium selection, and spatial sorting, and their social and political impacts and economic drivers.
